Caught in Paris’s Spell

Everything would be more fun with rumors and unconfirmed knowledge and discovery. Someone saying they heard that "at midnight all the ads in Times Square change to art" is so much more interesting and infinitely more fulfilling when you have to venture there to see if it’s true and it turns out to be real.

Or when you inevitably have questions about our world, instead of pulling out a phone and getting an answer that you will forget quickly and don’t fully understand, going to the library or the museum and researching.

I don’t enjoy seeing landscapes and cool places that much, even though I feel like I should. I think part of that is because most of the time seeing cool things nowadays consists of researching and finding these cool places on the web and then going to them, which just takes all the enjoyment out of it.

Can you imagine how enjoyable, how memorable, and fufilling it would be if you were exploring somewhere you had never been and you stumbled upon the most incredible castle that you didn’t know about before or a vast lake down some unknown path?

I think even if it was just unknown to you, it might be possible to get that feeling back in the days of the internet. So I vow to stop, as much as I can, always needing to know it all beforehand and being okay with maybe missing some stuff.

Sep 11, 2024.
