Caught in Paris’s Spell

I came to Paris expecting nothing more than spectacle, but I was wrong. it seems, that I’ve been won over by Paris—a turn of events I certainly didn’t foresee. I arrived ready to dismiss the city as all show and no substance, but somewhere between the twilight on the Seine and the rainy streets, it worked its charm on me.

I came here thinking it would be all show, no substance, but I couldn’t have been more wrong. What caught me first were the buildings, all aesthetic and none modern, such a dense cityscape somehow made me feel like i finally had room to breathe. There’s a sense of space, of safety, that I didn’t expect. And so many places to walk, even below these beautiful wide bridges are even wider somehow more lovely cobbles to walk upon, moving along with the saine.

The cigarette smoke? Awful to smell, but somehow it adds to the scene. There’s a strange elegance in the way it curls through the air. And the Eiffel Tower, of all things, wasn’t even cliché when I saw it lit up at night. It just felt... right, part of the fabric of the city.

Then there’s the way people dress here—effortlessly fashionable, like art is just something they wear. It’s in the air, this creative energy. I never thought a city could make me feel like staying, but here, it’s different. Paris feels like a place where you could stay for a year, work on something meaningful, and just be.

I didn’t expect to fall in love, but somehow Paris wrapped me up and left me wanting more.

It’s not just a city—it’s an atmosphere, one that almost begs you to take your time, to create, to live in the pauses. Maybe, just maybe, Paris is where I could let things unfold in their own time.

There’s a sense of history woven into the very streets—the knowledge that some of the greatest writers and artists walked here, letting the same rain fall on them, sitting by the Seine, imagining, creating. This was the creative hub of the world, and you feel it in every corner, on every rooftop, in the air itself.

Sep 11, 2024.
